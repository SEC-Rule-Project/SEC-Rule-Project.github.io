How long does it take to proposed rule to be finalized

Complexity between finalized rule and not finalized rule


The complexity is calculated by grade scale(from 1st grade to Nth grade) of Flesch Reading Ease score, The Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level, Gunning fog index, SMOG, Automated readability index, The Coleman-Liau Index, Linsear Write Formula, and Dale–Chall readability formula.
One grade scale of each metric is aggregated. The mode value(most frequent value) was chosen to represent comments complexity of each proposed rule.

Is comments complexity and the number of comments related?

This question might be helpful to understand analysis above because we all tend to mislead ourselve into something complicated is equals somethings a lot. However, Richard Feynman, a theoretical physicist, once said it’s not complicated, it’s many.
His quote describe which point we are baffled at. The massive amount of comments doesn’t mean that comments are complex to read. The below plot shows evidence.

As you can see here, those two variable(complexity and the number of comments) are not correlated(pearson correlation coefficient: -0.16).
